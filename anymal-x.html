Anymal X

Contributed to the development of ANYmal X, the world's first autonomous quadruped robot certified for operation in explosion-hazardous environments.

Field

Robotics

Period

2022 - 2025

Focus

Mechanical engineering / Systems engineering

The project

Development of world's first explosion-proof autonomous quadruped robot.

ANYmal X was developed to perform fully autonomous inspection missions in explosive environments, reducing human exposure while collecting reliable operational data. I contributed to the mechanical development, systems engineering and certification of the platform from early product development through industrialization.

As the project evolved, so did my role. What began with mechanical engineering gradually expanded into systems engineering, spanning mechanical integration, verification, requirements engineering and certification.

Key challenges

Engineering for hazardous environments.

The development required several mechanical and system-level challenges to be solved simultaneously.

  • Gas-tight enclosure design for Ex pxb protection
  • Integration of sensors
  • Battery system design
  • Ex d drive design
  • Weight reduction
  • Impact protection
  • Integration of safety devices

Reflection

What I took from the experience.

Building ANYmal X taught me that successful robotics is rarely about a single component. It emerges from the careful integration of mechanics, electronics, software and certification into one coherent system. Working across these disciplines fundamentally shaped how I approach engineering today.

It also reinforced the value of iteration: understanding a problem, testing an idea and improving it through observation.
